# Ways of organization

Snippets does not force you into a single organizational model. Instead, it offers multiple approaches that can be used independently or combined.

## Folders

Folders provide a familiar, hierarchical way of grouping related snippets. They allow content to be organized into nested structures that are easy to scan, reason about, and maintain over time. While hierarchical organization is sometimes criticized in personal knowledge management for being too rigid, folders continue to be highly effective for broad, stable structure. In Snippets, folders are treated as a supportive tool rather than a constraint: snippets can be placed into folders, folders can be nested, and icons can be assigned for visual orientation — offering clarity where it helps, without preventing more flexible forms of organization elsewhere.

## Dashboards

Dashboards offer a visual and flexible way of working. They provide a two-dimensional workspace where multiple elements can exist side by side, making it easy to keep related information together. Rather than relying on hierarchy, dashboards support spatial organization: content is arranged visually, and relationships emerge from layout and proximity. This makes dashboards especially well suited for overviews, projects, and workflows where seeing multiple pieces of information at once is valuable.

## Snippet references

Snippets can be linked together directly through references. References allow you to connect related pieces of information without placing them into a fixed hierarchy. This makes it possible to navigate quickly between ideas, follow chains of thought, and build a network-like structure where meaning emerges from connections rather than location. Folders, dashboards, and references are designed to complement each other: folders provide broad structure, dashboards support spatial organization, and references add flexible, contextual links between snippets.
